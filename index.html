Young Rewired State is a spin-off event from the Rewired State series. Born of a combination of improving young people's access to technology and engineering with Rewired States mission of openness, we present to you: the annual Festival of Code.

The event in 2012 runs from the 6th to the 12th August. During that week businesses, small and large, around the country act as hosts to local young people, YRS alumni, Rewired State mentors and other volunteers. You will be challenged to build digital products: mobile and web, using at least one piece of open data. It is free for every under 18 to enter. Here's what you'll need:

  • A laptop (any laptop you like, as long as you're comfortable using it)
  • At least a very basic understanding of programming, spreadsheets or graphics

At the end of the week everyone piles on coaches, trains and cars to the The Venue where they will have a night of camp and code, pizza and camaraderie.

On the Saturday afternoon, show off your creations to press, government and industry. Friends are made, lives are changed and, most importantly, prizes are given!

Our primary focus is to find and foster the young children and teenagers who are driven to teaching themselves how to code, how to program the world around them. This is a mighty challenge though well-supported with free tutorials online, but inevitably an isolating and solitary activity.

Join the community

After the event you're automatically a part of the growing network of young developers across the UK. Lifetime friendships are born as well as businesses. Mentor relationships are forged so you're no longer coding alone.

Who is Rewired State?

We run hack days. We take between 10 – 150 talented developers and give them money, time, space, caffeine, sugar and food, whilst they build cool/creative prototypes to solve your problems.
