ScraperWiki


ScraperWiki makes data *do stuff* across the web. We're used by data scientists all over the world to gather, store and visualise data. So, whether you're looking for trends on Twitter, wrangling some hairy public data, or building the next PlanningAlerts or OpenCorporates, ScraperWiki's the place to do it.

So, you're all awesome, and you're gonna have an amazing few days! But here are a few tips from the guys at ScraperWiki, to get you up and running quicker…

Pair! Find a buddy and work together. On everything. Take it in turns to be the Navigator (the one with the plan) and the Driver (the one doing the typing). You'll get so much more work done, with fewer bugs, and with better structure. Oh, and it's *way* more fun than hacking alone.

Always have a pen and paper on you. Complex functions and data structures are made simple and straightforward as soon as they're drawn on paper. It's magic. Especially when you've been coding for 10 hours straight and can't tell your keyboard from your elbow.

It's easy to get lost in code and lose sight of the end goal. Step away from the computer! Take a break. Talk to your users, and talk to your peers. Both will give you invaluable clues as to what's right or wrong about your current solution. And even if they don't, at least you've met someone new!

How to get help

Like I said earlier, don't be afraid to ask for help - people will be around to help. The most powerful thing about being at a hackday is that there will always be someone who knows what you don't, and more often than not - they will be happy to help.

Young Rewired State isn't just an event, it's a network of young people across the country. Before, during, and after the week of SAP Young Rewired State 2012, you will be able to ask for help from other participants, mentors, and well - me. Here's how you can get help throughout the week...

Mentors


In every centre, there will be at least one mentor, possibly several. These mentors don't just have a pretty face, almost all of them know at least one programming language. 'Why are these silly over 18s here?' you may be thinking - they are there to help you. You can ask them at any time for support.

What is Young Rewired State?

There is no end to the number of great resources out there to teach young people programming. While they are fantastic, learning is often an isolating and solitary activity. The aim for Young Rewired State is to give young people who have taught themselves to code a community of like-minded people.

On another note - hackdays are usually one to two day events where participants identify problems and rapidly create prototypes which demonstrate a range of solutions.

Young Rewired State is a week-long hack event for passionate under 19s. We’re now in our fourth year and SAP Young Rewired State 2012 will be held from Monday 6th to Saturday 11th of August 2012.

The underlying premise is simple – you have one week. Using at least one piece of open data, you must create at least one cool product or prototype (called ‘hacks’) that will be shown off to everyone else at the end of the week for a shot at some awesome prizes.

Young Rewired State is not designed to TEACH you how to code, it's designed to give young people who already know how to code a place to be. If you don't know how to code already, don't worry - there are some awesome resources to help teach you in this pack. Take time to go through them. No one will look down at you for having limited knowledge, they're there to help.
